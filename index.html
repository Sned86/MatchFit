About this project

Built for the
away day faithful

"Is there parking? What's the food like? Can you actually see from the terrace?" — the questions every groundhopper asks. We built the directory we always wished existed.

What this is

The Non-League Ground Directory is a community-powered reference for football fans who want honest, practical information about grounds across the English non-league pyramid — from the National League (Step 1) down through the regional feeder leagues at Steps 5 and 6.

Each club listing combines hard facts — ground name, town, county, league — with community ratings across four categories, and tags submitted by fans who've actually visited.

How ratings work

Every rating is submitted by a real person who has visited the ground. Ratings are scored out of 5 across four categories:

📍 Location & access
Ease of getting there — parking, public transport, walk from the station.
👋 Friendliness
How welcome away supporters feel — stewards, home fans, club staff.
🥧 Food & drink
Quality and range of catering — pies, drinks, real ale, anything on offer.
🏗️ Facilities
Terracing, cover, toilets, sightlines — the basics and beyond.

Who can contribute

Anyone. The directory is community-powered — it gets better the more people use it and add to it. You can:

  • Rate a ground you've visited
  • Suggest tags for a ground (pie van, real ale bar, dog friendly…)
  • Propose a correction to a club's static details
  • Submit a club we've missed

Scope

The directory covers clubs in the English non-league pyramid — Steps 1 through 6. This includes the National League, the Step 3 leagues (Isthmian, Northern Premier, Southern), and the regional feeders below.

Wales is not currently in scope. Only one Welsh club participates in the English non-league pyramid and the geographic complexity isn't worth it for now.

New clubs are being added regularly. Community features — ratings submission, tag suggestions, edit proposals — are coming soon.
